What is Acid–Base Titration?
Acid–base titration is a laboratory method used to determine the concentration of an unknown acid or base by reacting it with a standard solution of known concentration until the equivalence point is reached. The endpoint is detected using a suitable indicator. 

Important Terms
Term	Meaning
Titrant	Standard solution taken in burette
Analyte	Unknown solution taken in conical flask
Equivalence Point	Moles of H⁺ = Moles of OH⁻
End Point	Colour change of indicator
Indicator	Substance showing colour change near equivalence point
Remember: Endpoint ≈ Equivalence Point.

Neutralization Reaction
Acid + Base → Salt + Water

Example:

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Types of Acid–Base Titration
1. Strong Acid vs Strong Base
Example:

HCl vs NaOH

Characteristics:

Initial pH very low

Sharp rise near equivalence point

Equivalence pH = 7

Suitable indicators:

Phenolphthalein

Methyl Orange

2. Weak Acid vs Strong Base
Example:

CH₃COOH vs NaOH

Characteristics:

Initial pH higher than strong acid

Buffer region present

Equivalence pH > 7

Best indicator:

Phenolphthalein

3. Strong Acid vs Weak Base
Example:

HCl vs NH₄OH

Characteristics:

Equivalence pH < 7

Best indicator:

Methyl Orange

4. Weak Acid vs Weak Base
Example:

CH₃COOH vs NH₄OH

Characteristics:

No sharp pH change

Indicators are unreliable

Rarely asked in numerical titration problems.

Part A: Permanganometry (KMnO₄ Titration)
Theory Questions
1. Why is KMnO₄ called a self-indicator?
A. It changes colour only in alkaline medium

B. It has intense purple colour and excess KMnO₄ gives permanent pink colour

C. It is colourless

D. It reacts only with acids

✅ Answer: B

2. Why is dilute H₂SO₄ used instead of HCl in permanganometric titration?
A. HCl is expensive

B. HCl is volatile

C. Chloride ions are oxidized by KMnO₄

D. H₂SO₄ is stronger

✅ Answer: C

3. In acidic medium, KMnO₄ is reduced to
A. MnO₂

B. Mn²⁺

C. MnO₄²⁻

D. Mn

✅ Answer: B

4. Equivalent weight of KMnO₄ in acidic medium is
A. Molecular weight

B. Molecular weight/2

C. Molecular weight/5

5. Why is KMnO₄ solution not a primary standard?
A. Hygroscopic

D. Insoluble

KMnO₄ is a self-indicator due to its intense purple colour.

KMnO₄ is not a primary standard because it decomposes on standing and may contain MnO₂ impurities.

Oxalic acid titration is performed at 60–70°C because the reaction is slow at room temperature.
